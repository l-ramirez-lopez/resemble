Modelling using spectral data has garnered wide interest in the last two decades. Spectroscopy is the study of the spectral signature of material (e.g. soil, leaf, etc.). The spectral signature directly or indirectly relates to a wide range of chemical, physical of biological characteristics of the material under study. Spectroscopic measurements are fast, non-destructive and cost-efficient. The wide interest of spectroscopy has led to recent initiatives compiling large amount of spectral data, which increases de facto the complexity of the resulting datasets. To analyse spectral data, one must then resort numerical and statistical tools such as dimensionality reduction, and local spectroscopic modelling based the most similar spectra found in the dataset.

The resemble package contains functions for dimensionality reduction, computing spectral dissimilarity matrices, nearest neighbour search, and to fit local spectroscopic models. The core package functionalities include:

  • spectral dimension reduction
  • the computation of similarity measures
  • evaluation of dissimilarity matrices
  • spectral neighbour search
  • fitting local spectroscopic models
  • plotting results of modelling

NIRsoil <- NIRsoil[NIRsoil$CEC %>% complete.cases(), ]
wavs <- as.numeric(colnames(NIRsoil$spc))

NIRsoil$spc_p <- NIRsoil$spc %>% standardNormalVariate() %>% resample(wavs, seq(min(wavs), 
    max(wavs), by = 11)) %>% savitzkyGolay(p = 1, w = 5, m = 1)

train_x <- NIRsoil$spc_p[as.logical(NIRsoil$train), ]
train_y <- NIRsoil$CEC[as.logical(NIRsoil$train)]

test_x <- NIRsoil$spc_p[!as.logical(NIRsoil$train), ]
test_y <- NIRsoil$CEC[!as.logical(NIRsoil$train)]

sbl <- mbl(
  Xr = train_x, Yr = train_y, Xu = test_x,
  k = seq(50, 130, by = 20),
  method = local_fit_gpr(),
  control = mbl_control(validation_type = "NNv")
